Dr. Cristina Achim

Dr Cristina ACHIM (Popa) holds a PhD in physics since 2011, obtained at the University of Bucharest, Faculty of Physics. Currently, she works as a 2nd degree scientific researcher at the Department of Lasers, National Institute for Laser Plasma and Radiation Physics, Optics and Lasers in Life Sciences, Environment and Manufacturing Group and participates in a large number of research projects, being the scientific responsible/ coordinator of more projects in the last years, involving the development of infrared laser-based trace gas detection systems, photonic products and their applications in Life Sciences, including the application of laser spectroscopy technique for the detection of volatile organic compounds produced by plants, fruits and human breath.
Her expertise includes also setting up trials for testing and demonstration of optically based systems for detection of various gaseous molecules in both laboratory and other conditions (e.g. environmental, biology, medicine).
Her recent research interests mainly focus on laser applications in medicine & biology, photocatalysis, the interaction between microwave field and matter and plasma physics.
